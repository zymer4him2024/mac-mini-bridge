## Positioning summary (the message map)

These three messages should be consistent everywhere:

> **The category we play in:** Inbox-to-pipeline automation for B2B sales teams.
>
> **The "from / to":** *From* leads scattered across inboxes, spreadsheets, and CRMs your team forgets to update — *to* every relevant email automatically summarized, tracked, and deck-ready.
>
> **The unfair advantage:** Local AI (Ollama) runs on your hardware. Email content never leaves your perimeter. Same product, two deployment topologies (SaaS or on-prem) — chosen by the customer, not us.

The sales-led pitch leans on **time saved + leads not dropped**. The privacy-led pitch leans on **GDPR Article 44 + your inbox is yours**. Both are true. Audience picks the angle.

## Brand guardrails (summary)

Quick reference. The full reasoning lives in the [branding guide](14_branding_guide.md).

- **Tone:** Confident, calm, plain-spoken. No exclamation points unless quoting a customer. No "synergy," "leverage," "best-of-breed."
- **Color:** Recommended palette — accent `#1F4FFF` (deep blue), ink `#111111`, soft `#6B7280`, paper `#FFFFFF`, tint `#EEF2FF`. Pick one accent and use it everywhere.
- **Typography:** Inter for everything. Two weights only — Regular (400) and Bold (700).
- **Layout:** Whitespace > density. One idea per section.
- **Imagery:** Real product screenshots, never stock photos of people in headsets.

## What I left out (and why)

- **Investor deck.** Different audience, different narrative (TAM, traction, team). Build separately when you raise.
- **Full 40-page brand guidelines.** Premature for a small team. The [branding guide](14_branding_guide.md) gives you the working v0 (cheat sheet at the bottom of that file).
- **Whitepaper.** Lower priority for SMB sales — high effort, slow ROI. Write one when an enterprise prospect asks for it.
- **Video script.** Same reason — defer until your pitch is repeatedly closing.
- **Paid ads playbook.** Don't run paid ads before product-market fit. The launch plan covers this in Part 4.
