1. Can a non-technical user describe what changed in plain English? ("My leads stopped falling through the cracks.")

If both answers are no, the feature exists for the marketing page, not for the customer.

We try to hold ourselves to this. Our roadmap is "what outcome are we measurably moving?", not "what AI is shiny this month?"

## POST 9 — Industry observation

**Hook:** B2B sales tooling has had three eras:

**Era 1 — CRMs (1999–2010).** "Where do we store the data?"
**Era 2 — Engagement (2010–2020).** "How do we send more emails?"
**Era 3 — Intelligence (2020–today).** "What does the data mean?"

Era 3 is where AI inbox tools live. The question is no longer storage or volume. It's: of the 200 emails that hit your team's inbox this week, which 8 are real opportunities?

Most companies still answer that question by hand. Era 3 says you shouldn't.

## How to use this pack

- **Cadence:** One post per week. Mix problem-aware (1, 2), product-aware (3, 4), founder voice (5, 6), and opinion (7, 9). Save 8 for after you have a real customer quote. Save 10 for hiring moments.
- **Engage in the comments.** A post's reach doubles when the founder replies to the first 5 comments within an hour.
- **Repurpose to X.** Every post can become a 3–5 tweet thread. Hook becomes tweet 1; body becomes tweets 2–4; CTA becomes tweet 5.
- **Don't post a video the first 90 days.** Text-only posts outperform video on LinkedIn until you have brand recognition. Video is a vanity bet early on.

These posts are written assuming you (Shawn) are the public voice of the company. In B2B at this stage, the founder's account outperforms the company's account by 5–10x. Don't fight it — lean into it. The company account exists, but your personal account is the megaphone.
